History of the Bishop

The Bishop has its origins in Chaturanga, where it represented an elephant. As chess evolved in medieval Europe, it was transformed into its current form, moving diagonally across the board. The Bishop's long-range ability makes it a powerful piece for controlling squares and supporting attacks.

How the Bishop Moves

The Basic Movement

The Bishop moves diagonally across the board, covering squares in a straight line. It can move as far as possible in any diagonal direction, making it a versatile piece in both offense and defense.
