History of the King

The king is the centerpiece of chess, symbolizing the ruler of the kingdom. Throughout history, it has been the game’s most important piece. Losing the king means losing the game, making it the piece that the entire game revolves around.

In medieval Europe, the king’s role in the game reflected its importance in real-world monarchies. As the game evolved, so did the rules of the king, but its primary role remains the same—ensuring its survival.

How the King Moves

The Basic Movement

The king can move one square in any direction—vertically, horizontally, or diagonally. This allows it to move around the board, but it must always be careful, as it can be easily put in check.
